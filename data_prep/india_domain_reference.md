## Geography-bound occupations

- **MGNREGA**: RURAL ONLY (the "R" is Rural). Never pair with urban regions like Delhi NCR core, Mumbai metro, Bangalore city, Chennai, Kolkata, Chandigarh urban sectors.
- **Tea garden worker**: Assam, West Bengal (Dooars/Darjeeling), Tamil Nadu (Nilgiris), Kerala (Munnar/Wayanad). Never plains UP/Bihar/Rajasthan.
- **Salt pan worker**: Gujarat (Kutch), Tamil Nadu (Marakkanam), Andhra Pradesh coast.
- **Apple orchard worker/owner**: Himachal Pradesh, Jammu & Kashmir, Uttarakhand hill districts only.
- **Fisherman**: coastal states only — TN, Kerala, AP, Odisha, Maharashtra (coastal), Gujarat (coastal), West Bengal (Sundarbans area).
- **Gulf returnee**: most common from Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh, parts of UP. Rare from interior tribal districts.
- **Software engineer**: predominantly Bangalore, Hyderabad, Pune, Chennai, NCR, Mumbai. Possible in tier-2 cities (Indore, Coimbatore, Bhubaneswar). Unlikely in deep rural areas.

## Religion-specific naming guidance

- **Sikh**: men nearly always have "Singh" as last name (e.g., "Harpreet Singh"); women have "Kaur" (e.g., "Manpreet Kaur"). Family/clan names like Sandhu, Dhillon, Brar appear as middle or additional name.
- **Muslim (North India)**: common surnames Khan, Sheikh, Ansari, Siddiqui, Qureshi, Mansoori. First names: Mohammed, Abdul, Salim, Imran, Faisal (men); Fatima, Ayesha, Zareena, Nasreen (women).
- **Muslim (South India / Mappila)**: different surname patterns — Hajji, Koya, Musliyar, Thangal (Kerala); Sayed, Sait (Karnataka).
- **Christian (Kerala)**: Western/Hebrew first names + Syrian Christian surnames — George Mathew, Annamma Joseph, Thomas Varghese.
- **Christian (Goa)**: Portuguese-origin — D'Souza, Fernandes, Pereira, Rodrigues.
- **Christian (Northeast)**: anglicized first names + tribal surnames — David Murmu, Marina Deka, John Hansda.
- **Buddhist (Maharashtra Ambedkarite — largest group, ~7M)**: emerged from SC Dalit communities converting to Buddhism in 1956 following Dr. Ambedkar. Typically retain or modify SC-coded surnames: Gaikwad, Kamble, Sonawane, Salve, Khobragade, Meshram, Ramteke, Pawar (context). Buddhist first names increasingly used: Buddhapriya, Maitreyi, Bodhi, Dhamma, Arya, Tathagata, Tushar.
- **Buddhist (Northeast traditional)**: Sikkim, Arunachal Pradesh (Monpa, Sherdukpen, Khamba communities), some areas of Assam. Tibetan-Buddhist naming conventions: Tenzin, Sonam, Karma, Pema, Norbu, Dorjee, Lhamo, Choden. No Indian-style surnames; second name is often a personal name not a family marker.
- **Buddhist (Ladakh / Himachal Tibetan diaspora)**: similar Tibetan-Buddhist naming as NE. Tenzin/Sonam/Norbu/Karma/Pema/Dorjee plus place-based names.
- **Buddhist (traditional Indian Buddhists in Bihar)**: small Bodh community around Bodh Gaya — rare in synthetic data; if used, keep names neutral or Bodh-coded.

NEVER pair Buddhist persona with: Brahmin-coded surnames (Sharma, Mishra, Pandey, Tiwari, Mukherjee, Joshi, Iyer), Kshatriya-coded (Singh in UP/Bihar context, Thakur, Rajput), unless explicitly framed as a recent personal conversion case. Such pairings are extremely rare in real Indian Buddhist demographics and read as fictional.
